From Investigation to Innovation

I began my career in criminal investigation, where I learned to follow digital trails, reconstruct events, and think analytically under pressure. That experience shaped my mindset: methodical, curious, and deeply committed to uncovering truth — skills that later became essential in cybersecurity. Alongside this, I studied International Trade, gaining strategic thinking and fluency in English, which prepared me to operate across borders and disciplines. But my passion for technology kept growing, and I eventually transitioned into backend development. I specialized in Java and Spring Boot, building secure, scalable systems with clean architecture. Over time, I focused more deeply on cybersecurity, completing an intensive bootcamp BBK The Bridge. Currently, I’m finalizing a local AI application designed for privacy-first environments — no cloud, no external dependencies, just secure intelligence aligned with ethical standards. This project reflects everything I’ve learned: how to build, protect, and respect the systems we create. Soon, I’ll be certified in cybersecurity by the University of Oviedo, adding academic recognition to my hands-on expertise. My journey has never been linear — and that’s my strength. I bring together investigation, strategy, development, and security to build solutions that matter.

QuietLine (My Project)

This project explores the boundaries of local AI, where intelligence is deployed without dependence on external networks or cloud services. Built around a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) architecture, it operates entirely offline, drawing insights from internal sources while preserving full control over data flow and context. Designed for environments where privacy, security, and regulatory integrity are non-negotiable, the system offers a modular and extensible foundation for real-world use across sectors. It reflects a commitment to ethical technology—one that respects the user, protects the system, and delivers value without compromise. At its core, this is not just an application. It’s a statement: AI can be powerful, private, and principled.
